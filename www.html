A LOT TO TAKE IN

Stumbling into the world of music in 2015, Will Wood and his bandmates The Tapeworms produced Everything Is A Lot (2015) entirely of songs Will wrote between 2011 and 2014. The seeds of chaos and eccentricism seen throughout the Self-Ish era are planted.

NOT THE MOON AT NIGHT

Often accredited as the “Will Wood Gateway Drug” album with hit song Dr. Sunshine Is Dead, Self-Ish (2016) explores identity and the concept of self. Will completely lets loose as he sings, shrieks, and commands your attention with experimental instrumentation and lyricism that can only be described as “unhinged.”

SUBURBIA BASTARD

Good Morning! The Normal Album (2020) is chock full of doo-wops, blues, and bangers all critiquing the suburbia life style so often romanticized by Americans. Will Wood’s viral hit I/Me/Myself originates from this album, exploring gender identity and the purpose (or lack thereof) of labels.

[              ]

In Case I Make It (2022) is Will Wood's rawest, most intimate album to date. The Tapeworms make a return with some members taking up instruments they previously had not recorded with. It also features a full choir and more orchestral arrangements. Will opens the door to his psyche with a new brand of vulnerability.
